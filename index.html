We get Black and Latino children into rock climbing.


Black and Latino children have historically been left out of climbing.

Rock climbing has become an increasingly popular sport. Despite this increase in popularity, rock climbing has failed to become more diverse. In both of our climbing regions, we are one of the few Black / Latino climbers, despite there being 400+ competitors in each of our regions.

While we hope to cultivate a more welcoming environment in the climbing scene, we hope to inspire confidence in the next generation of athletes.


Who we are

We are Conrad Booker and Nyasha Knigge, two Black youth competitive climbers in high school at the Trinity School NYC.



Nyasha Knigge

Nyasha Knigge is a 17 year old competitive climber from Jersey City. She has been competing for 5 years in USAC Region 82.






Conrad Booker

Conrad Booker is a 17 year old competitive climber from NYC. He has been competing for 4 years in USAC Region 91.


What we have done so far!

Youth currently enrolled in RockRoots programming climb at the Cliffs at Harlem once a week, within the Cliffs' Saturday rec program. Along with the other coaches, Nyasha and Conrad work on foundational skills, such as team work and problem solving with the kids hands on.
